What is LTV?

Loan-to-Value explained using OnLock's 4 tiers as a live example.

LTV (Loan-to-Value) is the percentage of your collateral's value you can borrow. If you deposit $10,000 in BTC and choose a 65% LTV, you receive $6,500 in USDT — while keeping your BTC locked as collateral.

Higher LTV = more liquidity, but higher liquidation risk. If BTC price drops and your loan balance approaches your collateral value, OnLock sells collateral automatically to cover the debt.

OnLock offers 4 tiers — borrowers choose how much liquidity they need vs. how much safety margin they want. Lower LTV = safer, lower rate. Higher LTV = more cash out, higher rate.
